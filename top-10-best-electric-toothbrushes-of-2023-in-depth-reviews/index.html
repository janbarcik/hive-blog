FAQ Section

What makes an electric toothbrush better than a manual toothbrush?

Electric toothbrushes are generally more effective at removing plaque and reducing gingivitis due to their oscillating and rotating brush heads. They often come with features like timers and pressure sensors to ensure effective brushing.

How often should I replace the brush head of my electric toothbrush?

It's recommended to replace the brush head every three months or sooner if the bristles become frayed. Many electric toothbrushes have indicators to remind you when it's time to change the head.

Are electric toothbrushes suitable for children?

Yes, many brands offer electric toothbrushes designed specifically for children, with smaller brush heads and child-friendly features. Always supervise young children while brushing.

Can electric toothbrushes damage teeth or gums?

When used correctly, electric toothbrushes are safe for teeth and gums. It's important not to apply too much pressure and to use the recommended brushing technique.

Do I need to use special toothpaste with an electric toothbrush?

No, you can use any regular toothpaste with an electric toothbrush. However, some toothpaste brands offer formulations that complement electric toothbrushes for enhanced cleaning.
